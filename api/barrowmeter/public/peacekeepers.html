GAF Imposes New Payment Regime for Gambian UN Peacekeepers

March 27, 2018


Gambian soldiers and officers taking part in United Nations peacekeeping missions will no longer be laid in the dollar denomination, but the local Gambian currency the dalasi according to highly placed Gambia Armed Forces Command sources. The recent decision has caused uproar in the force as disgruntled soldiers accused military chiefs of exploitation.

Gambian soldiers are said to be among the most poorly paid soldiers around the world. The average private soldier earns barely less than $50 (fifty United States dollars) which is roughly 2'369.00 dalasis monthly salary. Peacekeeping is seen as a lucrative venture for Gambian security personnel. With the new payment regime imposed by the higher echelons of GAF, Gambian soldiers will now be paid in dalasi and not in dollars as it used to be obtained during the former regime. All sister forces in the country will affected by the new payment regime . Be it the police, the army, and tge intelligence community would be affected by the payment currency changes. According to GAF sources, soldiers were informed about the recent change in currency denomination. They so intimated that soldiers cleared to serve in UN peacekeeping missions will henceforth be bounded before they could take part in such missions. UN systems normally pay in dollars and as such the recent change has left many grasping for answers.

By: Ansumana S.O Nyassi
