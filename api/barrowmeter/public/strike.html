Teachers Condemn Education Ministry's Threats, Call For Continuation of Strike

February 07, 2018


Teachers on a sit-down strike in the Gambia have reacted angrily to the country's ministry of Basic and Secondary Education's warning for them to cut short their smack and report back to teaching duties or face punishment. The statement contained in a press release and copied to all media houses accused a group of individuals for starting the strike claiming they do not have the backing of the Teachers Union.

"These teachers are individuals and are not representing the entire teaching fraternity and do not have the backing of the Teachers Union which is the umbrella body representing teachers and all grievances should be reported to them for action".

The ministry further warned those on strike to report to work immediately or face sanctions. "As such the ministry is given them not less than 24 hours to report back to their normal teaching duties and failure to do so disciplinary actions will be taken against them accordingly".

Since its release, the ministry's press release has garnered strong rebuttals from many Gambians on social media. Reacting on his Facebook page, Gambian journalist and youth activist SaikouSuwarehJabai said " This Public Notice from the Ministry of Basic & Secondary Education on Teachers' Strike is utterly silly- complete nonsense!!!

Instead of engaging teachers on a discussion you are there sending threats, - shithole ministry"!!

Also reacting to the ministry's statement, MomodouSabally former Gambian Presidential Affairs minister and Head of Civil Service and one ModouLamin Sultan Jammeh both pointed to the poor grammar contained in the release saying "And the bad grammar is appalling" and "This is serious, unbelievable and unacceptable! The grammatical errors on this letter reveals that MOBSE has a lot do. Adiyamosiyaata (translate as too much talk in the local Mandinka dialect).

For his part, Baba S. Touray who is one of many teachers currently on strike urged his colleagues to continue on the strike until their conditions are met by government. " The threats issued by the Ministry of Basic and Secondary Education that teachers are given 24 hours to go back to schools and teach else drastic measures will be taken against them is another reason that makes me believed up to this day most government and public institutions in this country still live in the past. Threatened teachers is the last thing that the ministry should come up with. Come on teaching is the last option for most graduates, so those of us teaching deserves some respect.

By the way calling those on strike radicals only shows how weak your ministry is. How can you appoint people you labelled radicals to train and mentor future leaders? Couldn't the permanent secretary put the statement better than that? Thanks to the task force members and to the teachers currently on strike. At least our move is working well. The press release shows that we are winning and the ministry is getting too hot for them and they are scared. I urged all comrades to peacefully continue with the strike till when the Gambia Teachers Union, the Ministry of Basic and Secondary Education and the government show us the respect we deserve and are ready to meet our demands. To our colleagues that are still not with us, I want you all to understand that what we doing is in the interest of all of us and that it is a worthy cause to join. May God bless you all. Thanks"!

By: Ansumana S.O Nyassi
